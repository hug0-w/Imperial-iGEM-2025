GREAT EXHIBITION ROAD FESTIVAL

At The Great Exhibition Road Festival, we had the chance to introduce synthetic biology in a fun and informal way to young children, including kindergartners. To make molecular biology exciting and interactive, we teamed up with the Good Food Institute and co-organised a stall called Build a Microbial Factory.

We also featured The Overlab, a fast-paced, multiplayer game strongly inspired by Overcooked, but set in a synthetic biology lab instead of a kitchen. In Overlab, players take on different lab roles to build synthetic biology experiments under time pressure, from preparing reagents to assembling plasmids to running protocols. Just like in a real lab, teamwork and communication are essential to success. The game was a fun way for the children to learn about lab workflows and teamwork in science, in a fun, chaotic environment.

We taught kids aged 3 to 7 how a yeast cell functions using a hands-on marble game designed to simulate the journey of a molecule through a living cell. Children guided a marble which represents a protein or molecule, through a colourful, physical model of a yeast cell, passing through key organelles like the nucleus, endoplasmic reticulum, Golgi apparatus, and more. At each station, we explained what that part of the cell does in simple, relatable terms (like "this is where the cell reads instructions!" or "this is the cell’s post office!"). We turned complex molecular biology into an immersive experience that kids could touch, see, and understand.

Throughout the day, we welcomed 167 student players and received great feedback from both kids and parents about how much they enjoyed the experience.

YEAST COLOURING BOOK
View The Complete PDF

Synthetic biology and the greater STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics) field can be portrayed as too advanced or “grown-up,” leaving children out of the conversation and its role in shaping the future. We wanted to change that for our project, by creating a resource that introduces key concepts in a way that feels familiar, playful, and accessible to young audiences.

Our solution was to design an educational colouring book filled with themed pages that invite children to “meet the scientists,” imagine themselves doing lab work, and explore the building blocks of life, such as cells, organs, and even synthetic meat as part of our project. Each page was designed with simple, bold outlines for colouring, paired with short, child-friendly explanations that encourage curiosity and creativity.

Through combining classic colouring activities with real scientific themes, the book makes synthetic biology approachable at an early age, showing that science isn’t just something hidden away in labs but part of everyday life and future possibilities. Beyond being fun, it serves as a gentle introduction to synthetic biology, planting the seeds of interest in STEM while also helping children and their families feel included in scientific conversations.

Once complete, the colouring book was published online and presented in our visits to nurseries.

Task

  • Design and produce an engaging, child-friendly resource that introduces synthetic biology in a way that is both fun and educational.
  • Translate complex scientific concepts — such as cells, organs, lab work, and synthetic meat — into simple visuals and explanations that children could interact with through colouring activities.
  • Ensure that the book encouraged curiosity and creativity while remaining scientifically accurate and accessible to a non-specialist audience..
  • The resource had to be easy to distribute online and in our nursery visits.
